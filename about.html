About Us

Building bridges to better mental health for young people through
compassionate support, education, and community.

Know more

Little Minds was established in response to the growing mental health crisis affecting young people today. We recognized a critical need to build specialized support—support that is professionally guided and deeply empathetic to the unique challenges of adolescence, childhood, and young adulthood.

We are dedicated to moving beyond simple awareness. Our core purpose is to actively transform the mental health of young people, helping them feel better and receive the support they deserve. We work hard to ensure our care is reliable, our lessons are helpful, and that young people can easily ask questions and receive help.

Our Mission Statement

To fully empower the next generation by providing the support and tools they need to take control of their health, find help easily, and build a brighter future.

Values Guiding Our Work

Our values are the foundation of everything we do at Little Minds. They guide our approach, shape our programs, and ensure we provide the highest quality support to young people facing mental health challenges.

  1. Respect: We approach every individual with kindness and respect, listening without judgment. We honor each person's unique journey and create spaces where diverse perspectives and experiences are valued. Our team is trained in cultural competency to ensure we serve youth from all backgrounds with dignity.
  2. Accessible: We ensure our support is free and easy to access for all young people, regardless of financial situations. We offer multiple access points including in-person sessions, virtual counseling, text-based support, and community outreach programs. All our services are available in multiple languages and designed with neurodiversity in mind.
  3. Professionalism: We provide guidance backed by verified resources and trained professionals. Our team includes licensed therapists, certified counselors, and mental health specialists who participate in ongoing training. We follow evidence-based practices and maintain the highest ethical standards in all our interventions.
  4. Confidentiality: We maintain strict privacy standards to create a safe environment where youth can share openly. All personal information is protected, and we follow HIPAA-compliant procedures. Young people can trust that their stories and struggles remain confidential within the bounds of safety.
  5. Empowerment: We focus on building resilience and self-advocacy skills in young people. Rather than creating dependency, we equip youth with tools and strategies to navigate challenges independently. Our programs emphasize strength-building and help youth recognize their own capacity for growth and healing.
  6. Collaboration: We work together with families, schools, and communities to create comprehensive support systems. We believe mental health support is most effective when it involves multiple stakeholders who can provide consistent care and understanding across different environments.
  7. Innovation: We continuously evolve our approaches based on the latest research and youth feedback. Our programs incorporate creative therapies, technology solutions, and emerging best practices to meet the changing needs of today's youth in an increasingly complex world.
  8. Hope-Oriented: We maintain an unwavering belief in every young person's potential for healing and growth. Our approach focuses on strengths rather than deficits, possibilities rather than limitations, and always emphasizes that recovery and wellbeing are achievable goals.

Our Commitment to These Values

These values aren't just words on a page - they're living principles that guide our daily work. We regularly evaluate our programs against these standards, provide ongoing training to ensure our team embodies these values, and welcome feedback from the youth we serve about how we can better live up to these ideals.

Our Vision for Change

Our vision extends beyond immediate support to creating lasting, systemic change in youth mental health. Through strategic initiatives and community partnerships, we're building a future where every young person has access to the mental health resources they need to thrive.

  1. Working Together: Partnering with local groups to bring mental health support directly to communities.
    • Establishing satellite counseling centers in underserved neighborhoods
    • Training community leaders and school staff in mental health first aid
    • Creating peer support networks that extend our reach beyond clinical settings
    • Collaborating with religious institutions, youth centers, and local businesses to create mental health-safe spaces
  2. Creating Tools: Developing free guides and workshops led by experts to help young people learn and cope.
    • Digital mental health toolkit with coping strategies for anxiety, depression, and stress
    • Interactive workshops on emotional regulation, healthy relationships, and self-care practices
    • Parent and caregiver education programs to strengthen family support systems
    • School-based mental health curriculum integrated into existing educational frameworks
    • Mobile app with daily check-ins, crisis resources, and connection to support networks
  3. Speaking Up: Advocating for policies that make youth mental health a top priority.
    • Lobbying for increased government funding for youth mental health services
    • Campaigning for mental health education requirements in school curricula
    • Working with insurance providers to expand coverage for mental health treatments
    • Developing workplace mental health standards for youth employment programs
    • Creating youth advisory boards to ensure young voices shape policy decisions
  4. Building Resilience: Empowering youth with lifelong coping skills and emotional intelligence.
    • Mindfulness and meditation programs tailored to different age groups
    • Creative arts therapies that provide alternative expression channels
    • Physical wellness initiatives connecting mental and physical health
    • Leadership development programs that build confidence and self-efficacy
    • Career and life skills workshops that reduce future uncertainty anxiety
  5. Expanding Access: Removing barriers to mental health support for all young people.
    • Sliding scale and completely free counseling services based on need
    • Multilingual resources and culturally competent care providers
    • Telehealth options for rural and homebound youth
    • After-hours and crisis support available 24/7
    • Transportation assistance for in-person appointments
  6. Measuring Impact: Using data and research to continuously improve our approaches.
    • Regular outcome assessments to track participant progress
    • Youth feedback systems that directly influence program development
    • Partnerships with universities for ongoing research and evaluation
    • Transparent reporting of our impact to stakeholders and donors
    • Adaptive programming that evolves based on emerging needs and evidence
