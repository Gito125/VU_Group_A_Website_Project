What Affects Mental Health in Young People?

Social Pressure & Comparison

Constant pressure from peers, school, and social media can lead to anxiety, low self-esteem, and feelings of inadequacy. The curated perfection seen online creates unrealistic standards that can damage self-worth.

Relationships & Trauma

Exposure to trauma or unhealthy relationships can deeply affect emotional well-being. Adverse childhood experiences (ACEs) have been shown to significantly increase the risk of mental health challenges later in life.

Future Uncertainty

High expectations and fear of not meeting goals can cause stress, burnout, and depression. The pressure to make life-altering decisions about education and career paths during adolescence creates significant anxiety.

Biological Factors

Genetics, brain chemistry, and hormonal changes during puberty can play a significant role in mental health conditions. Understanding these biological components helps reduce stigma and promotes effective treatment.

Environmental Stressors

Living in unstable or unsafe environments, experiencing poverty, or facing discrimination can contribute to mental health challenges. Systemic issues create additional barriers to accessing care and support.

Academic Pressure

The demand to perform well in school, excessive homework, and high-stakes testing can be overwhelming and lead to anxiety, sleep disorders, and burnout among students.
